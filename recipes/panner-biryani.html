"Panner Biryani"

Description

Paneer Biryani is a delightful and aromatic rice dish that combines the rich flavors of spices with succulent paneer (Indian cottage cheese). This vegetarian variation of the classic biryani is perfect for special occasions or whenever you're craving a hearty and flavorful meal.

Ingridients

  • 200g panner, cut into cubes
  • 2 cups basmati rice, washed and soaked for 30 minutes
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 3-4 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon biryani masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt

Steps

  • Marinate the panner pieces in yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, biryani masala, garam masala, chopped coriander leaves, chopped mint leaves, and salt. Let it marinate for at least 1 hour in the refrigerator. Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil in a large pot. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Heat 2 tablespoons of ghee or oil in a large pot. Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and green chilies. Cook until the tomatoes turn mushy.
  • Add the marinated panner and cook until it is partially cooked, about 10 minutes.
  • In another pot, boil water and add soaked basmati rice along with salt. Cook the rice until it is 70% cooked. Drain and keep aside.
  • Layer the partially cooked rice over the panner in the pot.
  • Drizzle saffron milk over the rice for added flavor and color (optional).
  • Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes, until the panner is fully cooked and the rice is fluffy.
  • Once done, gently mix the rice and panner together.
  • Garnish with fried onions and chopped coriander leaves.
  • Serve hot with raita or salad. Enjoy your delicious panner biryani!
